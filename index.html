Hi, I am Md Mahabubul Alam, an electrical engineering PhD candidate at Pennsylvania State University, University Park (Penn State). I am currently working under the supervision of Dr. Swaroop Ghosh. I obtained my bachelors in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) in September 2015. I have worked as an ASIC Physical Design Engineer at Primesilicon Technologies, Bangladesh from November 2015 to December 2016. I joined Auburn University as a PhD student in January 2017. After spending a year at the department of electrical engineering at Auburn University, I moved to Penn State in January 2018. I briefly worked as an Interim Engineering Intern at Qualcomm, Bridgewater, NJ in the summer of 2018.

At Penn State, I am a member of the Lab of Green and Secure Integrated Circuit Systems. My primary research focus is on the Design Methodologies for Noise-resilient and High-performance Quantum Computing. Particularly, I am focusing on -

  • Noise-aware quantum circuit transpilation strategies for target hardware to maximize fidelity.
  • Training methodologies for Parameterized Quantum Circuits (PQC) to counter temporal and spatial qubit quality fluctuation induced performance degradation.
  • Acceleration of quantum-classical optimization algorithms through smart parameter initialization using machine learning.
  • Low-latency quantum circuit construction techniques.
